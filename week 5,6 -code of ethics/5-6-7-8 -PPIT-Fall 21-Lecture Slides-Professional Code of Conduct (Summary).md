# Professional Code of Conduct - Summary

## 1. Introduction to Professional Ethics

### What is Professional Ethics?
- Moral principles and standards guiding professional behavior
- For computing: ethical decision-making, responsibility to stakeholders, integrity, privacy, security

### Why It Matters
- Computing professionals impact millions of users
- Ethical failures cause harm, discrimination, privacy violations
- Professional reputation depends on ethical behavior
- Ethics go beyond legal requirements

### Key Concepts
- **Professionalism**: Competence, integrity, responsibility
- **Code of Ethics**: Written guidelines for acceptable behavior
- **Ethical Dilemmas**: Conflicting values or principles
- **Whistleblowing**: Reporting unethical/illegal activities
- **Conflict of Interest**: Personal vs. professional interests

## 2. Major Professional Codes

### ACM Code of Ethics
**General Principles:**
1. Contribute to society and human well-being
2. Avoid harm
3. Be honest and trustworthy
4. Be fair and non-discriminatory
5. Respect intellectual property
6. Respect privacy
7. Honor confidentiality

**Professional Responsibilities:**
- Achieve high quality in processes and products
- Maintain professional competence
- Know and respect existing rules
- Accept professional review
- Give comprehensive evaluations
- Honor contracts and agreements
- Improve public understanding
- Access resources only when authorized

**Leadership Principles:**
- Ensure public good is central concern
- Articulate and evaluate social responsibilities
- Manage resources to enhance quality of working life
- Create growth opportunities
- Care when modifying/retiring systems

### IEEE Code of Ethics
**Key Principles:**
- Accept responsibility for safety, health, welfare of public
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Be honest in claims/estimates
- Reject bribery
- Improve understanding of technology
- Maintain technical competence
- Seek and offer honest criticism
- Treat all persons fairly
- Avoid injuring others
- Assist colleagues' professional development

## 3. Ethical Issues in Computing

### Privacy and Data Protection
- Collect only necessary data
- Secure personal information
- Obtain informed consent
- Provide transparency
- Respect user rights (access, correct, delete)

### Security
- Implement robust security measures
- Report vulnerabilities responsibly
- Protect from unauthorized access
- Balance security with usability
- Ethical hacking considerations

### Intellectual Property
- Respect copyrights, patents, trademarks
- Proper attribution
- Understand open source licenses
- Avoid plagiarism
- Fair use considerations

### Accessibility
- Design for people with disabilities
- Follow standards (WCAG, Section 508)
- Test with diverse users
- Provide alternative access methods

### Quality and Reliability
- Thorough testing
- Honest reporting of capabilities/limitations
- Proper maintenance
- Adequate documentation
- Prompt error fixing

## 5. Ethical Decision-Making Frameworks

### Utilitarian Approach
- Maximize overall good / minimize harm
- Consider consequences for all stakeholders
- Challenge: Hard to measure outcomes

### Deontological Approach
- Focus on duties and rules
- Actions right/wrong regardless of consequences
- Challenge: Rules may conflict

### Virtue Ethics Approach
- Focus on character and virtues
- "What would a virtuous professional do?"
- Challenge: Different virtues for different people

### Rights-Based Approach
- Respect individual rights
- Protect fundamental rights
- Challenge: Rights may conflict

**Decision Process:**
1. Is it legal?
2. Is it ethical?
3. Does it align with professional code?
4. Will it harm anyone?
5. Can you justify it publicly?

## 6. Common Ethical Dilemmas

### Privacy vs. Security
- How much data collection is necessary?
- Can security be achieved with less invasion?
- Communicate trade-offs to users

### Innovation vs. Safety
- What are potential harms?
- How critical is the system?
- Can risks be mitigated after release?

### Employer vs. Public Interest
- Nature and extent of potential harm?
- Are there alternatives?
- When is whistleblowing appropriate?

### Open Source vs. Proprietary
- Licensing requirements
- Security implications
- Cost considerations
- Alignment with organizational values

## 7. Whistleblowing

### Definition
Reporting unethical, illegal, or harmful activities to appropriate authorities.

### When Justified
- Clear evidence of wrongdoing
- Internal channels exhausted
- Significant harm
- Proper motives
- Proportional disclosure

### Protections
- Legal protections (varies by jurisdiction)
- Professional code support
- Internal policies
- Anonymous reporting

### Risks
- Retaliation
- Career damage
- Legal consequences
- Personal/financial stress
- Social isolation

### Ethical Considerations
- Verify facts
- Use internal channels first
- Consider impact on innocent parties
- Ensure proper motives
- Follow proper procedures

## 8. Conflict of Interest

### Definition
Personal interests conflict with professional duties.

### Types
1. **Financial**: Personal gain vs. professional duty
2. **Personal relationships**: Friends/family in decisions
3. **Competing loyalties**: Multiple clients/employers
4. **Gifts and favors**: Benefits influencing judgment
5. **Outside employment**: Working for competitors

### Managing Conflicts
- Disclose potential conflicts
- Recuse from conflicting decisions
- Avoid conflict situations
- Follow organizational policies
- Seek guidance

### Examples in Computing
- Working for competitor
- Using company resources for personal projects
- Accepting vendor gifts
- Hiring friends/family improperly
- Investing in competing companies

## 9. Professional Liability

### Types
- Legal responsibility for actions
- Malpractice in services
- Negligence in design/implementation
- Product liability for defects

### Limiting Liability
- Professional liability insurance
- Clear contracts
- Proper documentation
- Following industry standards
- Regular professional development

### Responsibility Chain
- **Designers**: Initial design decisions
- **Developers**: Implementation quality
- **Testers**: Thorough testing
- **Managers**: Project oversight
- **Organizations**: Overall system safety

**Key Point**: Responsibility cannot be completely delegated; accountability extends to foreseeable consequences.

## 13. Case Studies

### Therac-25 Radiation Machine
- **Issue**: Software bug caused fatal overdoses
- **Failures**: Inadequate testing, poor documentation, no safety mechanisms
- **Lesson**: Thorough testing essential for safety-critical systems

### Volkswagen Emissions Scandal
- **Issue**: Software to cheat emissions tests
- **Failures**: Deliberate deception, environmental harm
- **Lesson**: Honesty in design, environmental responsibility

### Facebook-Cambridge Analytica
- **Issue**: Misuse of user data for political purposes
- **Failures**: Privacy violations, lack of consent, data misuse
- **Lesson**: Privacy protection, transparency in data use

## 14. Best Practices

### Daily Practices
- Follow professional codes
- Stay current with ethical issues
- Seek guidance for dilemmas
- Document ethical decisions
- Reflect on implications

### Facing Ethical Dilemmas
1. Identify the issue clearly
2. Gather relevant information
3. Identify stakeholders
4. Consider codes and laws
5. Evaluate using frameworks
6. Consult colleagues/committees
7. Make decision and document
8. Monitor outcomes

### Building Ethical Culture
- Lead by example
- Encourage ethical discussions
- Support ethical decision-making
- Recognize ethical behavior
- Address unethical behavior promptly

### Continuous Improvement
- Regular ethics training
- Stay informed about challenges
- Participate in professional development
- Contribute to discussions
- Update codes and standards
