## 1. ETHICAL THEORIES & 4-STEP PROCESS ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê (Q2 - 25 marks)

### Core Theories
| Theory | Key Point |
|--------|-----------|
| **Utilitarianism** | Greatest good for greatest number (consequentialist) |
| **Deontology** | Duty-based, Kant, rules not outcomes |
| **Egoism** | Self-interest based |
| **Hedonism** | Pursuit of happiness |
| **Consequentialism** | Focus on outcomes |
| **Rights & Duties** | Individual rights + moral duties |

### 4-Step Framework ‚≠ê

**STEP I: Understanding**
- List 10-15 facts (numbered)
- Identify 4-6 ethical issues (why + harm)
- List 8-10 stakeholders

**STEP II: Dilemma**
- "Should X do A or B?"

**STEP III: Analysis (BOTH ALTERNATIVES)**
- **Consequentialism (A-G)**: Who harmed/benefits? Greatest good?
- **Rights/Duties (1-5)**: What rights/duties? Which take precedence?
- **Deontology (H-P)**: Universalization test, equal treatment, respect

**STEP IV: Decision & Implementation**
- Clear decision (reference frameworks)
- 5-8 implementation steps
- Stakeholder impacts
- 4-6 preventive measures (tech/org/legal/cultural)
- Pivot point prevention

### Key Definitions
- **Whistleblowers**: Unauthorized disclosures about harm
- **Moral principles ‚â† Law**
- **Code objectives**: Discipline, Inspiration, Education (NOT Enforcement)

## 2. BUSINESS ORGANIZATIONS (Q3 - 15 marks)

| Feature | Sole Proprietorship | Public Ltd. | SMC Pvt. Ltd. |
|---------|-------------------|------------|---------------|
| **Owners** | 1 | 7+ (unlimited) | 1 |
| **Directors** | N/A | 3+ | 1 (+ 2 nominees) |
| **Legal Entity** | No | Yes | Yes |
| **Liability** | Unlimited | Limited | Limited |
| **Suffix** | None | Limited (Ltd.) | (SMC-Private) Limited |
| **Prospectus** | No | Required | Not allowed |
| **Secretary** | No | Required | Required (director ‚â† secretary) |

**Veil of Incorporation**: Separates company from owners (limited liability)

**Contract Clauses**:
- **Non-compete**: Can't work for competitors
- **Non-solicitation**: Can't poach employees/customers

## 3. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE (Q6 - 10 marks)

### Types
| Type | Description | Best For |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Simple** | Few layers, central decision | Small startups |
| **Functional** | By departments (HR, Tech, Sales) | Mid-sized |
| **Divisional/Product** | By products/clients | Product-focused |
| **Matrix** | Dual reporting (functional + project) | Complex projects |
| **Flat** | Minimal hierarchy, wide span | Innovation (DPL, Netflix) |
| **Hierarchical/Tall** | Many levels, clear chain | Traditional (Banks) |

### Key Elements
- **Work Specialization**: Division of tasks
- **Departmentalization**: Grouping (function/product/geography)
- **Chain of Command**: Who reports to whom
- **Span of Control**: Wide (autonomy) vs Narrow (control)
- **Centralization**: Where decisions made
- **Formalization**: Degree of rules

### Factors Affecting Structure
Strategy, Size, Technology, Environment, Culture/Leadership, Geography, Legal

## 6. BUSINESS MODELS (Q1: Q1, Q20, Q24)

**Key Definitions**:
- **Revenue Model**: Framework for generating income
- **Business Model**: Overall approach to creating/delivering/capturing value
- **Value Proposition**: Promise of value to customer

**Common Models**:
- **Freemium**: Free basic + paid premium
- **Donationware**: Supported by donations
- **Crippleware**: Limited version to encourage purchase
- **SaaS/Subscription**: Recurring fees
- **Advertising**: Free to users, revenue from ads
- **Marketplace**: Connects buyers/sellers (commission)

---

## 7. COMPUTER CRIMES & PECA (Q1: Q17, Q18, Q19 | Q4: Q4b, Q4c)

**Computer Crime**: Tool, target, or storage medium for offence

**Business Attacks Purpose** (Q4b - 3 marks):
1. Disrupt operations
2. Steal data
3. Cause financial harm
4. Gain competitive advantage

**Jurisdiction Challenges** (Q4c - 3 marks):
1. Borderless nature (multiple countries)
2. Which country's laws apply?
3. Extradition challenges
4. Anonymous attackers (VPN, TOR, crypto)
5. Cryptocurrency payments

**PECA 2016 Penalties**:
- **Harassment**: 3-7 years, Rs. 1-10M
- **Fraud/Identity**: Months to 7 years, fines
- **Cyber Terrorism**: Up to 14 years, Rs. 50M

**Key Terms**:
- **Data Mining**: Analyzing data for patterns
- **Collaborative Filtering**: Form of data mining (recommendations)
- **Micro Targeting**: Using data mining to target groups

## 9. CONTRACTS (Q4: Q4d, Q4e)

**Indemnity** (Q4d - 3 marks):
- Contractual obligation to compensate
- Protection against liability
- Risk transfer mechanism

**Cost-Plus vs Fixed Price** (Q4e - 3 marks):
- **Cost-Plus**: Pay actual costs + profit (risk to client)
- **Fixed Price**: Agreed price regardless (risk to contractor)

## 10. LEGAL SYSTEM (Q1: Q8, Q13, Q14, Q15)

**Key Definitions**:
- **Jurisdiction**: Area covered by single legal system
- **Succession**: Falls under **Civil Law**
- **Court System**: Civil courts fall under High Courts (**True**)
- **Legislative Process**: National Assembly + Senate

**Hierarchy**: Supreme Court ‚Üí High Courts ‚Üí District Courts ‚Üí Special Courts

## 11. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY (Q1: Q6)

| Type | Protects | Duration |
|------|----------|----------|
| **Copyright** | Expression (code, UI, docs) | Life + 50 years |
| **Trademark** | Brand (names, logos) | Renewable |
| **Patent** | New, inventive inventions | 20 years |
| **Trade Secret** | Confidential info | As long as secret |

**Key Points**:
- Copyright protects **expression, NOT ideas**
- Trademark can be domain name (use carefully)
- Unauthorized copying = unethical + illegal

**Core Principles**:
1. Fair & lawful processing
2. Purpose limitation
3. Data minimization
4. Accuracy
5. Storage limitation

**Security**: RBAC, MFA, Encryption (transit + rest), Audit trails, Backup/recovery

## 18. GROUP DYNAMICS

**Tuckman's Stages**: Forming ‚Üí Storming ‚Üí Norming ‚Üí Performing ‚Üí Adjourning

**Decision Techniques**: Brainstorming, Nominal Group, Delphi, Consensus, Majority Vote, Authority

**Groupthink**: Illusion of invulnerability, pressure for conformity, self-censorship

**Communication Channels**: Rich (face-to-face) ‚Üí Moderate (email) ‚Üí Lean (memos)

## 19. CONFLICT & NEGOTIATION

**Conflict Styles** (Thomas-Kilmann):
- **Competing**: High assert, low cooperate
- **Accommodating**: Low assert, high cooperate
- **Avoiding**: Low both
- **Collaborating**: High both
- **Compromising**: Moderate both

**BATNA**: Best Alternative To Negotiated Agreement (determines reservation point)

**Negotiation Types**: Distributive (zero-sum) vs Integrative (win-win)

---

## 20. LEADERSHIP & MENTORSHIP

**Management vs Leadership**:
- Management: Processes, efficiency, stability, short-term
- Leadership: People, vision, change, long-term

**Leadership Styles**: Autocratic, Democratic, Laissez-Faire, Transformational, Transactional, Servant, Situational

**EI Components**: Self-awareness, Self-regulation, Motivation, Empathy, Social skills

**Mentorship Types**: Formal, Informal, Peer, Reverse, Group, Virtual

## Q2 Strategy (Ethics - 25 marks)
1. **Read scenario** (5 min)
2. **Step I** (10 min): 10-15 facts, 4-6 issues, 8-10 stakeholders
3. **Step II** (2 min): Frame dilemma
4. **Step III** (15 min): **ALL 3 frameworks for BOTH alternatives**
5. **Step IV** (13 min): Decision, steps, impacts, prevention, pivot point

**Common Mistakes**: Missing frameworks, vague steps, not analyzing both alternatives

## Q3 Strategy (Structures - 15 marks)
- Define each structure
- Compare side-by-side
- Include advantages/disadvantages

## Q4 Strategy (Short Answers - 15 marks)

- Be concise but complete
- Provide examples
