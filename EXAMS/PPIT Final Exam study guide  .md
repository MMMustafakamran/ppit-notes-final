### 1. ETHICAL THEORIES AND FRAMEWORKS [Q1: Q2, Q5, Q7, Q9 | Q2: Entire Question - 25 marks]

**Critical Concepts:**
- **Utilitarianism**: "Greatest good for the greatest number" - consequentialist theory focusing on outcomes and collective welfare
- **Deontology**: Duty-based ethical theory attributed to Emmanuel Kant - focuses on duties and rules, not consequences
- **Egoism**: Self-interest based (NOT duty-based or altruism-based)
- **Hedonism**: Pursuit of happiness as highest ethical aim
- **Consequentialism**: Ethical theory focusing on outcomes (Utilitarianism is a type)
- **Rights and Duties**: Ethical framework considering individual rights and moral duties

**4-Step Ethical Decision Making Process:**
1. **Step I: Understanding the Situation**
   - List relevant facts
   - Identify ethical issues and potential harm
   - List stakeholders

2. **Step II: Isolating the Major Ethical Dilemma**
   - Identify the core ethical conflict to resolve

3. **Step III: Analyzing Ethicality**
   - Using Consequentialism (outcomes)
   - Using Rights and Duties
   - Using Deontology (duties/rules)

4. **Step IV: Making Decision and Implementation**
   - Make defensible ethical decision
   - List implementation steps
   - Show stakeholder impacts
   - Suggest longer-term preventive changes

**Key Points:**
- Ethical theories are NOT formulas but frameworks for analysis
- Moral principles ‚â† Law (they can differ)
- Whistleblowers make unauthorized disclosures about harmful situations
- Code of conduct objectives: Discipline, Inspiration, Education (NOT Enforcement)

**Chapters to Focus:**
- Ethical theories and frameworks
- Ethical decision-making models
- Professional codes of conduct (ACM)

### 2. BUSINESS MODELS AND REVENUE MODELS [Q1: Q1, Q20, Q24]

**Critical Concepts:**
- **Revenue Model**: Framework for generating income (NOT the same as business model)
- **Business Model**: Overall approach to creating, delivering, and capturing value
- **Value Proposition**: The promise of value to be delivered to a customer (NOT the same as business model or revenue model)
- **Freemium**: Some features free, advanced features require payment
- **Donationware**: Software supported by donations
- **Crippleware**: Limited version to encourage purchase of full version
- **Commission Model**: Revenue from commissions on transactions

**Chapters to Focus:**
- Business Models in IT
- Revenue models
- Value proposition

### 3. BUSINESS ORGANIZATIONS AND STRUCTURES [Q1: Q21, Q22 | Q3: 15 marks]

**Critical Concepts:**
- **Veil of Incorporation**: Legal principle that separates company from its owners (limited liability)
- **Sole Proprietorship**:
  - Single owner
  - Not separate legal entity
  - Unlimited liability
  - Simple setup
  - Personal tax

- **Public Limited Company (Ltd.)**:
  - Shares traded publicly
  - 7+ members, unlimited maximum
  - 3+ directors
  - Separate legal entity
  - Limited liability
  - Prospectus required
  - Public subscription allowed

- **Single Member Company (SMC Pvt. Ltd.)**:
  - One shareholder/director
  - Separate legal entity
  - Limited liability
  - Must end with "(SMC-Private) Limited"
  - Requires nominee director
  - Requires company secretary (sole director cannot be secretary)

**Employment Contract Clauses:**
- **Non-compete Clause**: Prevents employees (current and former) from working for direct competitors for a period
- **Non-solicitation Clause**: Prevents employees from encouraging other employees or customers to move to another company

**Chapters to Focus:**
- Business Organizations
- Company structures in Pakistan
- Legal entities

### 7. LEGAL SYSTEM OF PAKISTAN [Q1: Q8, Q13, Q14, Q15]

**Critical Concepts:**
- **Jurisdiction**: Area covered by a single legal system and set of laws
- **Succession**: Falls under Civil Law
- **Court System**: 
  - Civil courts fall directly under High Courts (True)
  - Hierarchy: Supreme Court ‚Üí High Courts ‚Üí Civil Courts
- **Legislative Process**: National Assembly and Senate (NOT Superior judiciary or three pillars)

### 9. COMPUTER CRIMES AND CYBER SECURITY [Q1: Q17, Q18, Q19 | Q4: Q4b, Q4c]

**Critical Concepts:**
- **Data Mining**: Process of analyzing data to discover patterns
- **Collaborative Filtering**: Form of data mining (used in recommendations)
- **Micro Targeting**: Using data mining to target specific groups (e.g., political campaigns)
- **Business Attacks**: Purpose is to disrupt operations, steal data, cause financial harm, gain competitive advantage
- **Jurisdiction Challenges in Internet Crime**:
  - Borderless nature (victim, criminal, servers in different countries)
  - Which country's laws apply?
  - Extradition challenges
  - Different legal standards
  - Anonymous attackers
  - Cryptocurrency payments hard to trace

### 10. PROFESSIONAL LICENSING AND CERTIFICATION [Q1: Q23 | Q4: Q4a]

**Critical Concepts:**
- **Licensing**: Process by which candidates are evaluated to determine readiness to enter profession
- **Purpose of Licensing**:
  - Ensure minimum competency
  - Protect public interest
  - Regulate profession
  - Maintain standards
  - Legal authorization to practice

- **Certification**: Voluntary recognition of skills/knowledge
- **Accreditation**: Recognition of educational institutions/programs

**Chapters to Focus:**

### 11. CONTRACTS [Q4: Q4d, Q4e]

**Critical Concepts:**
- **Indemnity**: Contractual obligation to compensate for loss or damage
  - Protection against liability
  - Financial compensation
  - Risk transfer mechanism

- **Cost-Plus Contracts**:
  - Client pays actual costs + profit margin
  - Example: Software development where client pays for hours worked + overhead + profit percentage
  - Risk: Costs can escalate
  - Benefit: Flexibility for changes

- **Fixed Price Contracts**:
  - Agreed price regardless of actual costs
  - Example: Software project for $50,000 fixed price
  - Risk: Contractor bears cost overruns
  - Benefit: Predictable cost for client

**Chapters to Focus:**
- Contracts and legal agreements
- Business practices

- **Copyright Violation**: Making unauthorized copies or planting viruses is NOT ethical and NOT legal

**Chapters to Focus:**
- Ethics and professional conduct
